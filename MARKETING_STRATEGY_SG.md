## High-Impact Free Channels (Singapore-Specific)

### 1. Reddit — r/singapore (Priority: HIGH)
- Post a "I built this" thread in r/singapore — the sub is very active and loves locally-built tools
- Frame it as: "I built a free app to help SG drivers document accidents properly — no sign-up, works offline"
- Engage genuinely with comments, accept feedback
- Post again when you ship major updates

### 2. HardwareZone Forums (Priority: HIGH)
- **HWZ Car Talk subforum** is the biggest SG driver community
- Create a thread introducing the app, explain the pain point (24h SAS deadline, people panicking at the scene)
- HWZ users love free tools and will spread word-of-mouth

### 3. Facebook Groups (Priority: HIGH)
- **SG Road Vigilante** (~600k members) — share as a useful tool, not promotion
- **Singapore Drivers** and **SG Car Community** groups
- **Grab/Gojek driver groups** — they deal with accidents frequently
- Post with a story: "I got into an accident and realized there was no good app for this, so I built one"

### 4. WhatsApp / Telegram (Priority: HIGH)
- Your OG tags are now set up — when someone shares your URL in WhatsApp/Telegram, it will show a proper preview card
- Ask friends and family to share in their group chats
- Create a short message template they can forward:
  > "Free app for SG drivers — helps you document accidents step by step, take the right photos, and check if you can use GIA fast track. Works offline too. [URL]"

### 5. TikTok / Instagram Reels (Priority: HIGH)
- **"What to do after a car accident in Singapore"** — educational content performs extremely well
- Script idea (30-60 seconds):
  1. Hook: "Most SG drivers don't know they have 24 HOURS to file an accident statement"
  2. Show the pain: panicking at the scene, forgetting to take photos
  3. Show the app: quick demo of the guided flow
  4. CTA: "Free app, no sign-up, link in bio"
- Film it at a carpark or with a car in frame for authenticity
- Use hashtags: #sgdrivers #singaporedriving #caraccidentsg #sglife #adulting

### 6. YouTube Shorts (Priority: MEDIUM)
- Same content as TikTok, cross-post
- Also create a longer 3-5 min tutorial: "How to properly document a car accident in Singapore"
- This ranks well on Google and YouTube search

### 7. Carousell (Priority: MEDIUM)
- List it as a free resource in the "Cars" or "Services" category
- People browse Carousell for car-related things in SG

### 8. LinkedIn (Priority: MEDIUM)
- Post about building it — the SG tech community on LinkedIn is supportive
- Frame it as a side project story / civic tech
- Tag it with #buildinpublic #madeInSG #civictech

### 9. Driving Schools & Instructors (Priority: MEDIUM)
- Contact CDC, SSDC, BBDC driving centres — offer it as a free resource for new drivers
- New drivers are the most anxious about accidents and most likely to adopt a tool
- Ask instructors to recommend it

### 10. Insurance Agents (Priority: MEDIUM)
- Reach out to motor insurance agents (they're everywhere in SG)
- They can recommend it to clients as a value-add — it actually helps with claims documentation

## Viral Mechanics

- **The app itself is shareable** — the "Share Report" feature means every user potentially shows it to the other party, witnesses, and their insurer
- **Accident clusters** — when someone uses it, they tell their friends "download this before you need it"
- **Driving test milestone** — target the moment someone passes their driving test ("Now that you have your license, download this just in case")
